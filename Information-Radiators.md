# Team Celebrations and Recognitions (Let's Celebrate Our Wins!)
For this section, I wanted to create a space that truly celebrates our team's hard work and achievements. Here's what I came up with:
Showcasing Victories:
-	"High Fives" Wall: I propose dedicating a physical wall or a digital space (like a shared Google Sheet) as our "High Fives" Wall.  Here is the sample google sheet: 
[MDMA-HighFivesWall-Sample.xlsx](https://1drv.ms/x/s!AmkPT8Vm30OXhcRPnAg4WY42reTz4g?e=0ijMYJ)
Here, we can acknowledge individual and team accomplishments. For example, if someone tackles a particularly complex bug, we can add their name and a brief description of their accomplishment. We can also recognize team wins, like completing a challenging sprint goal or exceeding our test coverage target.
-	"Rockstar of the Week": Each week, we can anonymously nominate a teammate who has gone above and beyond. This could be someone who consistently helps others, demonstrates exceptional problem-solving skills, or brings a positive and supportive attitude to the team. We can announce the "Rockstar" on our team communication channel (Slack or Teams) and may be even include a small token of appreciation, like a gift card or team lunch (depending on our budget).
Encouraging Peer Recognition:
-	"Shout-Out" Channel: I think it would be great to create a dedicated channel on our communication platform (Slack) specifically for acknowledging teammates like #mdma_shoutout. This could be a simple text channel where anyone can post a quick message recognizing someone's contribution.
-	Digital Stickers: Building on the "Shout-Out" channel idea, we could explore implementing a system of digital stickers. These could be fun, custom-designed stickers related to our project or specific skills (e.g., "Bug Buster," "Code Master," "Teamwork Champ"). Team members could award these stickers on the "Shout-Out" channel to acknowledge each other's contributions.
Celebrating Milestones:
-	Milestone Markers: As we reach major project milestones, I propose creating a visual representation, like a marker on a physical timeline or a progress bar on our digital information radiator. This will not only track our progress but also serve as a source of pride for the team.
-	Milestone Party: For particularly significant milestones, we could consider a small team celebration, either virtual or in-person (depending on school guidelines). This could be a quick pizza party or a virtual hangout to acknowledge our accomplishment and boost team morale.
By implementing these ideas, I believe we can create a culture of appreciation and recognition within our team. It will motivate us to keep pushing forward, celebrate each other's successes, and ultimately, achieve our project goals.

Meetings and Getting in Touch:
