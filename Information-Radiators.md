## Task-4 : Cumulative flow diagram

- Assigned To : Brijitha Tialu
- Explain :
**Purpose:**
The Cumulative Flow Diagram (CFD) component on our information radiator serves to provide a visual representation of the project's workflow dynamics, depicting the status and progress of work items throughout the project lifecycle. By analyzing the CFD, our team can identify bottlenecks, monitor workflow efficiency, and make informed decisions to optimize project delivery.

**Metrics Included:**

**Backlog Items:**

**Description:** This metric reflects the total number of backlog items, including user stories, features, and tasks, awaiting implementation.
**Objective:** Keep the backlog items within manageable limits to ensure a smooth flow of work and prevent accumulation.

**In Progress Items:**

**Description:** This metric indicates the number of items currently in progress, actively being worked on by the team.
**Objective:** Maintain a balanced workload by limiting the number of simultaneous in-progress items to prevent overloading team members.

**Completed Items:**

**Description:** This metric represents the total number of items completed and delivered during each reporting period.
**Objective:** Increase the rate of completed items to ensure continuous delivery and progress towards project milestones.

**Workflow Analysis:**

**Backlog Refinement Sessions:**

**Description:** This metric tracks the frequency and effectiveness of backlog refinement sessions aimed at grooming and prioritizing backlog items.
**Objective:** Conduct regular backlog refinement sessions to ensure that backlog items are well-defined, prioritized, and ready for implementation.

**Adherence to Work in Progress (WIP) Limits:**

**Description:** This metric monitors the team's adherence to predefined WIP limits for each stage of the workflow, such as development, testing, and review.
**Objective:** Enforce WIP limits to prevent work overload, maintain flow efficiency, and identify areas of improvement in workflow processes.

**Lead Time and Cycle Time:
Lead Time:**

**Description:** This metric measures the total time taken from the creation of a backlog item to its completion and delivery.
**Objective:** Minimize lead time by optimizing workflow processes, reducing dependencies, and streamlining communication channels.

**Cycle Time:**

**Description:** This metric calculates the average time taken to complete individual backlog items from start to finish.
**Objective:** Optimize cycle time by identifying and addressing bottlenecks, improving task estimation accuracy, and promoting collaboration within the team.

**Instructions for Maintaining and Updating the Information Radiator:**

**Regular Review Sessions:** Schedule regular review sessions to analyze the CFD and identify trends or patterns in workflow dynamics.
**Data Updates:** Ensure that the CFD is updated with the latest data on backlog items, work in progress, and completed items at regular intervals.
**Team Communication:** Share insights from CFD analysis with team members to foster collaboration, address issues, and make data-driven decisions.
**Documentation:** Document observations, trends, and actions resulting from CFD analysis for future reference and continuous improvement.

**Conclusion:**
The Cumulative Flow Diagram component plays a crucial role in providing visibility into the project's workflow, facilitating better decision-making, and driving continuous improvement. By monitoring key metrics and analyzing workflow dynamics, our team can optimize processes, enhance productivity, and deliver successful outcomes. Regular updates and effective communication are essential for maximizing the value of the CFD and ensuring project success.

## Task-8 : Key performance indicators (KPIs) for the project

- Assigned To : Brijitha Tialu
- Explain :
**Purpose:**
The Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) component on our information radiator aims to provide insights into the project's performance against predefined goals and objectives. By monitoring these KPIs, our team can assess progress, identify areas for improvement, and ensure alignment with the project's strategic direction.

**Metrics Included:**
**Donor Engagement Rate:**

**Description:** This metric measures the rate at which material donors are converted into monetary contributors. It indicates the effectiveness of our communication and engagement strategies.
**Calculation:** (Number of donors who made monetary contributions / Total number of material donors) * 100
**Objective:** Achieve a donor engagement rate of at least 20% within the next quarter.

**Email Conversion Rate:**

**Description:** This metric tracks the percentage of recipients who make monetary contributions after receiving email communications.
**Calculation:** (Number of donors who made monetary contributions after receiving emails / Total number of email recipients) * 100
**Objective:** Increase email conversion rate by 15% within the next six months.

**Program Enrollment Rate:**

**Description:** This metric measures the rate at which donors enroll in specific programs after receiving communication.
**Calculation:** (Number of donors enrolled in programs / Total number of donors contacted) * 100
**Objective**: Achieve a program enrollment rate of 25% within the next quarter.

**Average Donation Amount:**

**Description:** This metric calculates the average monetary contribution made by donors.
**Calculation:** Total monetary contributions / Number of donors who made contributions
**Objective:** Increase the average donation amount by 10% within the next year.

**Integration Status:
Integration of Email Automation Platform:**

**Description:** This metric tracks the status of integrating an email automation platform for sending targeted communications to donors.
**Objective:** Complete integration by the end of the current sprint.

**Deployment of Donor Tracking System Updates:**

**Description:** This metric monitors the deployment status of updates to the donor tracking system, including features aimed at improving donor engagement.
**Objective:** Deploy updates without disruptions to service by the end of the month.

**User Interaction Metrics:
User Engagement with Program Information:**

**Description:** This metric measures user interaction with program information on the donor tracking system.
**Objective:** Increase user engagement by 20% through UI enhancements and targeted communications.

**Feedback and Satisfaction Ratings:**

**Description:** This metric tracks user feedback and satisfaction ratings collected through surveys or feedback forms.
**Objective:** Maintain a satisfaction rating of at least 4 out of 5 based on user feedback.

**Instructions for Maintaining and Updating the Information Radiator:**

**Regular Review:** Schedule regular review sessions to assess the current status of KPIs and integration efforts. Determine if any updates or changes are needed based on progress made.
**Data Updates:** Assign a team member responsible for updating KPIs with the latest data and integration status. Ensure accuracy and timeliness of updates.
**Communication:** Maintain open communication channels within the team to report changes or developments related to KPIs and integration efforts. Encourage timely updates to ensure accurate representation.
**Documentation:** Document any changes or updates made to KPIs and integration efforts. Keep a record of past performance for reference and tracking.

**Conclusion:**
By monitoring these KPIs and integration efforts, our team aims to track project progress, identify areas for improvement, and ensure alignment with project objectives. Regular updates and effective communication are essential for maintaining the accuracy and relevance of the information radiator.
