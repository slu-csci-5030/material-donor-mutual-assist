## Task-11: Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

- Assigned To : Ajay Kumar Medikonda
- Explain :
Risk assessment and mitigation strategies are crucial aspects of our project management approach for the material donor mutual assist project. We have conducted a thorough analysis to identify potential risks that could impact project success and have developed comprehensive strategies to address them effectively. Here's a detailed overview of our risk assessment and mitigation strategies:

1. **Technical Risks**:
   - **Dependency on Third-Party Services (AWS, others)**:
     - **Risk**: There is a risk of service outages or disruptions with third party services like AWS, which could affect our email notification module.
     - **Mitigation**: To mitigate this risk, we have also discussed other possible alternatives for this and lso possible ways to continue with aws for long time. Additionally, we regularly monitor service status and have automated alerts in place to notify us of any issues.

2. **Resource Constraints**:
   - **Limited Expertise in NATS and AWS**:
     - **Risk**: Some team members may have limited expertise in technologies like NATS and AWS, which could lead to implementation challenges or delays.
     - **Mitigation**: To address this risk, we have provided possible docs to learn and resources to upskill team members in these technologies. Additionally, we have established clear communication in slack and whatsap groups adn also in retro meetings for seeking assistance or guidance from more experienced team members or external experts.

3. **Security Vulnerabilities**:
   - **Data Breaches or Security Incidents**:
     - **Risk**: There is a risk of potential security breaches or data leaks, especially with sensitive donor information stored in our PostgreSQL database.
     - **Mitigation**: We have implemented robust security protocols, including encryption of sensitive data, regular security audits, and access controls to restrict unauthorized access to the database. We also stay updated on security best practices and promptly apply security patches and updates to mitigate known vulnerabilities.

4. **Operational Risks**:
   - **Data Loss or Corruption**:
     - **Risk**: There is a risk of data loss or corruption, particularly with the PostgreSQL database storing critical project data.
     - **Mitigation**: To mitigate this risk, we have implemented regular backup procedures, automated database backups to secure cloud storage, and disaster recovery plans to restore data in case of emergencies. Additionally, we conduct routine database maintenance tasks to optimize performance and minimize the risk of data loss.

5. **External Dependencies**:
   - **Third-Party Integration Issues**:
     - **Risk**: Our project relies on various third-party integrations, such as email notification services and external APIs, which could lead to integration issues or disruptions.
     - **Mitigation**: We closely monitor third-party service status and API changes, maintain clear communication channels with service providers, and have contingency plans in place to adapt to any changes or disruptions quickly.

Overall, our risk assessment and mitigation strategies are designed to proactively identify and address potential risks, ensuring the successful delivery of our material donor mutual assist project. By implementing these strategies, we aim to minimize project disruptions, safeguard project data and resources, and maintain the integrity and reliability of our project throughout its lifecycle.
